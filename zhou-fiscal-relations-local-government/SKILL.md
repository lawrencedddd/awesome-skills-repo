## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Fiscal Sociology Lens**: Treat fiscal relations as social relations among central government, local governments, departments, firms, peasants, banks, and public-service units. Do not stop at budget categories; trace interaction, bargaining, dependency, and discretion.
- **Government-as-Relation Model**: Split "government" into levels and departments. A county, township, education bureau, finance bureau, land reserve center, and financing platform can have different information, incentives, and constraints.
- **Fiscal Contracting and Local State Corporatism**: Under fiscal contracting (财政包干), local governments kept more marginal revenue and became entrepreneurial, using "raise fish in water" (放水养鱼) strategies to grow enterprises and fiscal income.
- **Tax-Sharing Reform as Revenue Recentralization**: The 1994 tax-sharing reform (分税制) standardized and centralized revenue while many expenditure responsibilities remained local. This created fiscal pressure and redirected local incentives.
- **Revenue Squeeze and Behavioral Displacement**: When formal budgetary revenue becomes centralized and tightly managed, local governments search for revenue sources outside strict central control: fees, funds, land conveyance revenue, platform financing, and construction-related taxes.
- **Transfer Payment Dependence**: Transfers reconnect central revenue to local spending, but earmarked transfers make local governments look upward for projects and approval rather than downward toward local needs.
- **Earmarking / Projectization 专项化**: Special-purpose funds improve formal traceability but fragment local discretion and can weaken service delivery when upper-level departments lack grassroots information.
- **Suspended Grassroots Regime 悬浮型政权**: After tax-fee reform, some township governments stopped extracting from peasants but also lost fiscal capacity and service responsibilities, leaving them organizationally present yet socially detached.
- **Land Finance 土地财政**: Land conveyance revenue creates a "second finance" outside ordinary budgetary revenue; it also feeds the first finance through construction and real-estate tax bases.
- **Land-Fiscal-Financial Trinity 三位一体**: Land collateral enables bank credit; credit funds expropriation and urban construction; construction raises land values and fiscal revenue; revenue supports more borrowing. This loop explains fast land urbanization and its risks.
- **Fiscal Tournament 财政锦标赛**: Local governments compete through fiscal gain, visible construction, and quantifiable achievements. The danger is that government starts taking fiscal gain as its own end: 以利为利.
- **Measurability Trap**: What is easy to count, inspect, and rank (GDP, revenue, roads, buildings, land sales) can crowd out what is harder to measure (service quality, fairness, welfare, rural embeddedness).
