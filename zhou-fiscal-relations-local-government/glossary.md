# Glossary

**以利为利**: Treating fiscal gain as the guiding end of government action, rather than subordinating fiscal capacity to public purpose. (Ch 15)
**财政包干 (fiscal contracting)**: A fiscal arrangement in which local governments bargain over base revenue and retain some or all revenue above the contracted amount. (Ch 3)
**分税制 (tax-sharing reform)**: The 1994 reform that divided taxes between central and local governments, centralizing major revenue sources while many spending duties remained local. (Ch 4)
**财政锦标赛 (fiscal tournament)**: Competition among local governments to generate fiscal revenue, growth, and visible performance under centralized personnel control. (Ch 1, Ch 15)
**放水养鱼**: A strategy of nurturing enterprises or tax bases first so future fiscal revenue grows. (Ch 3)
**收入集权、支出分权**: The mismatch in which revenue is centralized upward while expenditure responsibility remains substantially local. (Ch 4)
**转移支付 (transfer payments)**: Funds transferred from upper-level governments to lower-level governments to fill fiscal gaps, equalize services, or pursue upper-level priorities. (Ch 5)
**专项化 (earmarking/projectization)**: The channeling of fiscal funds into designated projects or purposes, increasing formal control but often reducing local flexibility. (Ch 8)
**县乡财政危机**: Fiscal crisis at county and township levels caused by weak own-source revenue, payroll pressure, public-service duties, and constrained transfers. (Ch 6)
**悬浮型政权 (suspended grassroots regime)**: A township-level regime detached from peasants after losing both extractive tasks and substantive public-service capacity. (Ch 7)
**空壳化 (hollowing-out)**: The condition in which an organization remains formally present but loses effective fiscal discretion and governing capacity. (Ch 7)
**土地征用 (land expropriation)**: Administrative conversion of rural collective land into urban construction land under state authority. (Ch 12)
**土地财政 (land finance / land fiscalism)**: Local fiscal dependence on land conveyance revenue and land-related taxes, often forming a second fiscal system. (Ch 13)
**第二财政 (second finance)**: Land-centered funds outside ordinary budget revenue that local governments can use for construction and development. (Ch 13)
**土地金融 (land financialization)**: The use of land, expected land revenue, and government-backed platforms as collateral or support for credit. (Ch 14)
**三位一体 (land-fiscal-financial trinity)**: The development loop linking land, fiscal revenue, and finance into a self-reinforcing urbanization model. (Ch 14)
**地方融资平台 (local financing platform)**: Government-linked company used to finance infrastructure and land development through debt and collateral. (Ch 14)
**经营城市 / 经营土地**: Local-government practice of treating urban construction and land development as fiscal and growth strategies. (Ch 13, Ch 14)
