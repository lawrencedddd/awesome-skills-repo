# Chapter 2: Central-Local Relations before Reform

## Core Idea
Pre-reform fiscal relations created a recurrent cycle: centralization secured control, while decentralization was used to mobilize local initiative.

## Mental Models
- Think of fiscal institutions as **relations among actors**, not just rules on paper.
- Use **money flow as behavior flow**: where funds come from changes where officials look, bargain, and report.
- Separate **formal control** from **substantive control**: tighter documents can coexist with more creative local workaround behavior.

## Anti-patterns
- **Government as one actor**: misses conflicts between center, province, county, township, and bureaus.
- **Policy intention reading**: assumes the stated purpose of a reform is the same as its behavioral consequence.
- **Revenue-only analysis**: ignores expenditure responsibility, personnel control, land authority, and project channels.

## Worked Example
A campaign-style push can rapidly transmit targets down the hierarchy, but if success is measured by political performance rather than material feasibility, local governments may exaggerate output and hide failure.

## Key Takeaways
1. Local activism existed before market reform; reform changed its fiscal payoff structure.
2. High mobilization capacity can generate growth or disaster depending on feedback and constraints.
3. Personnel centralization is the hidden background of fiscal behavior.
