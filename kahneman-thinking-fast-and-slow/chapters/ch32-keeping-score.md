# Chapter 32: Keeping Score

**Book context:** Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow*.  
**Section:** Part IV: Choices

## Core Idea
Mental accounts, sunk costs, and regret shape decisions by assigning outcomes to psychological ledgers.

## Frameworks Introduced
- mental accounting
- sunk-cost fallacy
- regret
- disposition effect

## How to Apply It
1. Name the intuitive answer or emotional pull before accepting it.
2. Identify the statistical or structural information the intuition may be ignoring.
3. Convert the judgment into a slower procedure when the stakes, reversibility, or uncertainty justify the effort.
4. Record the reason for the decision before the outcome is known.

## Typical Examples
- selling winners while holding losers; staying in a bad project to avoid booking a loss

## Anti-Patterns
- Treating a coherent story as if it were reliable evidence.
- Ignoring sample size, base rates, incentives, feedback quality, or missing data.
- Confusing confidence, fluency, vividness, or emotional intensity with accuracy.

## Takeaways
- Close mental accounts deliberately.
- Treat sunk costs as information about learning, not a reason to continue.
