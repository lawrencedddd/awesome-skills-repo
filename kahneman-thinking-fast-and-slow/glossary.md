# Glossary

## System 1

Fast, automatic, associative, effortless cognition that generates impressions, feelings, and impulses.

## System 2

Slow, effortful, deliberative cognition used for calculation, rule following, monitoring, and self-control.

## WYSIATI

What You See Is All There Is: the tendency to build coherent judgments from available evidence while neglecting missing evidence.

## Cognitive ease

The feeling of fluency, familiarity, and comfort that can be mistaken for truth or quality.

## Substitution

Answering a difficult target question by unconsciously replacing it with an easier heuristic question.

## Anchoring

A bias in which an initial number or value pulls later estimates toward itself.

## Availability heuristic

Judging frequency or probability by the ease with which examples come to mind.

## Representativeness

Judging probability by similarity to a stereotype or prototype, often neglecting base rates.

## Base-rate neglect

Underweighting prior probabilities when vivid individuating information is present.

## Conjunction fallacy

Judging a combined event as more probable than one of its broader components.

## Regression to the mean

The statistical tendency for extreme observations to be followed by less extreme ones.

## Planning fallacy

Underestimating time, cost, and obstacles by relying on the inside view of a project.

## Outside view

Forecasting from outcomes of similar cases before adjusting for the specific case.

## Prospect theory

A descriptive theory of choice under risk based on reference points, diminishing sensitivity, loss aversion, and probability weighting.

## Loss aversion

The tendency for losses to affect value more strongly than equivalent gains.

## Endowment effect

The tendency to value owned goods more than equivalent unowned goods because giving them up is coded as a loss.

## Fourfold pattern

The pattern of risk attitudes across high/low probabilities and gains/losses.

## Narrow framing

Evaluating a decision in isolation rather than as part of a broader portfolio or repeated class.

## Mental accounting

Organizing gains, losses, costs, and regrets into separate psychological accounts.

## Peak-end rule

The remembering self's tendency to evaluate episodes by their most intense moment and their ending.

## Focusing illusion

The error of overestimating the importance of whatever currently occupies attention.
