# Patterns and Interventions

## Pattern: The Story Feels Complete

**Likely mechanism:** WYSIATI, halo effect, confirmation bias, narrative coherence.  
**Intervention:** List missing facts, alternative explanations, and disconfirming evidence before deciding.

## Pattern: The First Number Has Gravity

**Likely mechanism:** anchoring and insufficient adjustment.  
**Intervention:** Produce independent estimates, use ranges, and delay exposure to external anchors when possible.

## Pattern: A Vivid Example Dominates the Discussion

**Likely mechanism:** availability heuristic and affect heuristic.  
**Intervention:** Convert examples into frequencies, denominators, and comparison classes.

## Pattern: A Person or Case "Looks Like" a Category

**Likely mechanism:** representativeness and base-rate neglect.  
**Intervention:** Start with the prior probability and update only for reliable diagnostic evidence.

## Pattern: A Small Sample Looks Meaningful

**Likely mechanism:** law of small numbers.  
**Intervention:** Check sample size, expected variance, and confidence intervals before explaining the pattern.

## Pattern: A Forecast Is Built From the Inside View

**Likely mechanism:** planning fallacy and optimism bias.  
**Intervention:** Use reference-class forecasting and a premortem.

## Pattern: Expert Confidence Is Persuasive

**Likely mechanism:** illusion of validity.  
**Intervention:** Ask whether the domain has valid cues, rapid feedback, and a verified track record.

## Pattern: A Loss Feels Much Larger Than an Equal Gain

**Likely mechanism:** loss aversion and reference dependence.  
**Intervention:** Identify the reference point and test whether the same choice looks different from another neutral baseline.

## Pattern: Each Risk Is Evaluated Alone

**Likely mechanism:** narrow framing.  
**Intervention:** Adopt a risk policy for repeated decisions and evaluate the portfolio.

## Pattern: A Past Cost Keeps a Bad Project Alive

**Likely mechanism:** sunk-cost fallacy and mental accounting.  
**Intervention:** Ask whether you would invest fresh resources today if the past cost had not occurred.

## Pattern: Equivalent Options Produce Different Choices

**Likely mechanism:** framing effect.  
**Intervention:** restate outcomes in gains, losses, survival, mortality, percentages, and natural frequencies.

## Pattern: Happiness Is Judged by a Single Focal Factor

**Likely mechanism:** focusing illusion.  
**Intervention:** ask how much attention the factor will receive during ordinary daily life.
