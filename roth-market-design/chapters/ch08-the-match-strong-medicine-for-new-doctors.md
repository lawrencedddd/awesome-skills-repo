# Chapter 8: The Match: Strong Medicine for New Doctors

            ## Core Idea
            The medical residency market shows how a clearinghouse can repair unraveling and instability. The National Resident Matching Program works by collecting rank-order lists and using an algorithm designed to produce stable, safer matches.

            ## Key Concepts
            - **Thickness**: Enough participants gather together to create meaningful opportunities.
            - **Congestion**: Participants cannot evaluate or act on opportunities quickly enough.
            - **Safety**: Participants can use the market without excessive risk or strategic distortion.
            - **Rules**: The formal or informal design features that shape behavior.
            - **Strategic behavior**: Participant adaptation to incentives created by the rules.

            ## Mental Models
            - Use **the market as a designed institution** when the current outcome seems natural but is rule-dependent.
            - Use **participant safety** when deciding whether the mechanism helps ordinary users or only sophisticated ones.
            - Think in **failure modes first**: thickness, congestion, safety, legitimacy, and logistics.

            ## Anti-patterns
            - **Centralizing without incentive safety**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.
- **Ignoring couples or special constraints**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.
- **Treating the algorithm as the whole design**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.

            ## Worked Example
            In deferred acceptance, applicants propose to their top programs. Programs hold their most preferred applicants up to capacity and reject others. Rejected applicants propose to their next choices. Tentative holds become final when no proposals remain.

            ## Key Takeaways
            1. Clearinghouses solve timing and stability problems together.
2. Stable matching matters because unstable outcomes invite side deals.
3. Algorithm design must be fitted to the real market's constraints.
