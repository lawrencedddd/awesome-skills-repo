# Chapter 11: Repugnant, Forbidden . . . and Designed

            ## Core Idea
            Markets are constrained by social values as well as efficiency. A transaction may be mutually desired by participants but still forbidden or resisted because others find it repugnant.

            ## Frameworks Introduced

            ## Key Concepts
            - **Thickness**: Enough participants gather together to create meaningful opportunities.
            - **Congestion**: Participants cannot evaluate or act on opportunities quickly enough.
            - **Safety**: Participants can use the market without excessive risk or strategic distortion.
            - **Rules**: The formal or informal design features that shape behavior.
            - **Strategic behavior**: Participant adaptation to incentives created by the rules.

            ## Mental Models
            - Use **the market as a designed institution** when the current outcome seems natural but is rule-dependent.
            - Use **participant safety** when deciding whether the mechanism helps ordinary users or only sophisticated ones.
            - Think in **failure modes first**: thickness, congestion, safety, legitimacy, and logistics.

            ## Anti-patterns
            - **Efficiency-only persuasion**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.
- **Ignoring nonparticipants**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.
- **Assuming repugnance is fixed**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.

            ## Worked Example
            Selling a kidney for cash is illegal in many countries and repugnant to many citizens. Kidney exchange avoids the cash sale by matching incompatible donor-patient pairs, preserving donation while increasing transplants.

            ## Key Takeaways
            1. Market design must account for social acceptance.
2. The presence of money can transform how a transaction is judged.
3. Repugnance can block markets and guide alternative designs.
