# Chapter 10: Signaling

            ## Core Idea
            When communication becomes cheap, attention becomes scarce and messages lose credibility. Signals help markets by letting participants convey serious interest or relevant quality in a limited, interpretable way.

            ## Key Concepts
            - **Thickness**: Enough participants gather together to create meaningful opportunities.
            - **Congestion**: Participants cannot evaluate or act on opportunities quickly enough.
            - **Safety**: Participants can use the market without excessive risk or strategic distortion.
            - **Rules**: The formal or informal design features that shape behavior.
            - **Strategic behavior**: Participant adaptation to incentives created by the rules.

            ## Mental Models
            - Use **the market as a designed institution** when the current outcome seems natural but is rule-dependent.
            - Use **participant safety** when deciding whether the mechanism helps ordinary users or only sophisticated ones.
            - Think in **failure modes first**: thickness, congestion, safety, legitimacy, and logistics.

            ## Anti-patterns
            - **Unlimited expressions of interest**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.
- **Ambiguous signal meaning**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.
- **Creating signaling arms races**: It fails because the rule rewards behavior that weakens market function.

            ## Worked Example
            If students can apply easily to many colleges, colleges may struggle to know who would actually attend. A limited set of interest signals can help students identify a few schools where their interest is especially strong.

            ## Key Takeaways
            1. Cheap communication creates congestion and weakens meaning.
2. Signals work when they are limited, costly, or otherwise credible.
3. A good signal should route attention without becoming wasteful.
