# Chapter 15: The Threat of the Impossible

## Core Idea
In deep slump, policymakers can remain trapped by fear of inflation even when the present danger is collapse. Galbraith reads this as the continuing power of monetary memory over contemporary judgment.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Past trauma blocking present response**:
  - When to use: when leaders fear the wrong disaster.
  - How: compare current macro conditions with the historical memory dominating debate.
- **Fear as economic force**:
  - When to use: when caution becomes self-defeating.
  - How: track how fear changes behavior and deepens contraction.

## Key Concepts
- Policy hesitation
- Inflation fear
- Recovery blockage

## Mental Models
- Monetary memory can be maladaptive when conditions reverse.
- Impossible outcomes become politically potent if people vividly remember them.

## Anti-patterns
- Fighting inflation in a deflationary environment.
- Assuming caution is always safer than intervention.

## Worked Example
Galbraith links Depression-era hesitation to prior inflation anxieties, showing how yesterday's monetary nightmare can obstruct today's rescue.

## Key Takeaways
1. Policy errors often come from misreading the current danger.
2. Fear is a transmission mechanism in monetary crisis.
3. The memory of inflation can make depression management harder.
