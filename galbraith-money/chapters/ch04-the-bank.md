# Chapter 4: The Bank

## Core Idea
The bank can create prosperity and delusion at the same time. Galbraith uses John Law and early central-banking episodes to show that credit expansion is productive until confidence breaks.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The bank as miracle and menace**:
  - When to use: when reading booms financed by credit.
  - How: hold together the gains from liquidity and the dangers of speculation.
- **Confidence-driven expansion**:
  - When to use: when banks seem to create wealth effortlessly.
  - How: ask how much depends on continued faith in redemption and asset values.

## Key Concepts
- John Law
- Speculative finance
- Reckoning after expansion

## Mental Models
- Bank-created money feels harmless until many holders test convertibility at once.
- Financial genius and financial cupidity can be hard to separate in real time.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating every boom as proof of durable wealth creation.
- Mistaking temporary market enthusiasm for permanent monetary stability.

## Worked Example
Law's experiment shows the entire cycle in compressed form: easier money, warmth and expansion, then the painful realization that promises outran the structure supporting them.

## Key Takeaways
1. Credit can create genuine short-run prosperity.
2. Speculation thrives when monetary expansion feels effortless.
3. Monetary breakdown often starts as overconfidence.

## Connects To
