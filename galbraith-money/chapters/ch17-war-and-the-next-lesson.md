# Chapter 17: War and the Next Lesson

## Core Idea
War again demonstrates that rigid orthodoxy dissolves under necessity. It also proves that large-scale public management of money, credit, and output can succeed when political will exists.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **War as macroeconomic demonstration**:
  - When to use: when asking whether governments can mobilize idle resources.
  - How: study wartime production and finance as evidence against fatalistic passivity.
- **Catastrophe as teacher**:
  - When to use: when doctrines change only after extreme events.
  - How: identify what wartime administration made undeniable.

## Key Concepts
- War finance
- Full employment mobilization
- Policy learning

## Mental Models
- States discover capacities in emergency that peacetime orthodoxy had denied.
- Lessons learned in war often reshape peacetime economic management.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating wartime economic success as irrelevant to civilian management.
- Forgetting how much doctrine changes once idle capacity must be used quickly.

## Worked Example
Galbraith uses war not to celebrate conflict but to show that the old claim of helplessness in the face of depression was intellectually weakened by actual performance under mobilization.

## Key Takeaways
1. Large-scale coordination can defeat stagnation.
2. War exposed the practical weakness of passive orthodoxy.
3. Postwar monetary order carries wartime lessons inside it.
