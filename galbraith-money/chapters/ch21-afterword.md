# Chapter 21: Afterword

## Core Idea
Galbraith closes by arguing that the system could be better than it is. The afterword reinforces his skepticism toward monetary mystique and his view that the personnel and institutions governing money often deserve less reverence than they receive.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Demystify the guardians**:
  - When to use: when experts or institutions claim special exemption from ordinary judgment.
  - How: inspect their record, incentives, and plain competence.
- **Money as governable craft**:
  - When to use: when the field is treated as too mysterious for democratic scrutiny.
  - How: bring monetary questions back to institutions, choices, and consequences.

## Key Concepts
- Expert mystique
- Institutional competence
- Reform possibility

## Mental Models
- Monetary governance should be intelligible enough to criticize.
- Failure often survives by hiding behind complexity and status.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating money management as occult knowledge.
- Assuming poor performance is inevitable because the subject is difficult.

## Worked Example
The afterword acts as a final restatement of method: the book is a history of recurrent human error made plausible by status, doctrine, and mystery.

## Key Takeaways
1. Better monetary governance is possible.
2. Mystery often protects mediocrity.
3. Historical understanding is a practical tool, not just retrospective commentary.
