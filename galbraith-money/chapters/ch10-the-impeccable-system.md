# Chapter 10: The Impeccable System

## Core Idea
Galbraith examines the Federal Reserve with deliberate irreverence. Institutions celebrated as sophisticated and stabilizing can still embody blind spots, prestige, and doctrinal failure.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Prestige versus performance**:
  - When to use: when a monetary institution is treated as beyond criticism.
  - How: compare its reputation to what it actually prevented or failed to prevent.
- **Technical mystique**:
  - When to use: when monetary governance seems inaccessible by design.
  - How: restate the institutional task plainly: preserve liquidity, confidence, and stability.

## Key Concepts
- Federal Reserve
- Institutional reverence
- Monetary management

## Mental Models
- Complexity can conceal accountability.
- Admiration from experts is not evidence of public success.

## Anti-patterns
- Mistaking procedural elegance for actual competence.
- Outsourcing judgment entirely to official prestige.

## Worked Example
Galbraith's target is not expertise itself but the tendency to surround monetary authorities with an aura that discourages ordinary historical scrutiny.

## Key Takeaways
1. Central-bank dignity should not suspend criticism.
2. Monetary institutions are political actors with consequences.
3. The test is always crisis performance.
