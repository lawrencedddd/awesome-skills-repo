# Chapter 19: The New Economics at High Noon

## Core Idea
Keynesian and post-Keynesian ideas reach maximum policy prestige, even when the name Keynes remains politically awkward. Galbraith reads this as the moment when active macro management feels normal and successful.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Keynesianism without the label**:
  - When to use: when policy adopts the substance of intervention while avoiding the ideological banner.
  - How: inspect what officials actually do, not the names they avoid.
- **Peak doctrine confidence**:
  - When to use: when a policy framework seems to have solved the problem set it inherited.
  - How: ask which vulnerabilities are being overlooked because success feels settled.

## Key Concepts
- New Economics
- Full employment policy
- Managed demand

## Mental Models
- Policy ideas can win operationally before they win rhetorically.
- Periods of greatest confidence often contain the next blind spot.

## Anti-patterns
- Taking mid-century success as final proof that the problem of money was solved.
- Confusing political caution about names with absence of substantive change.

## Worked Example
Galbraith presents the high noon of the New Economics as impressive but not immune to later contradiction. Success changes the problem set rather than ending it.

## Key Takeaways
1. Keynesian methods became mainstream through practice.
2. Apparent resolution creates new forms of complacency.
3. Doctrine at its peak is hardest to question.
