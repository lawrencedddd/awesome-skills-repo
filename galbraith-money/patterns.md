# Patterns

## Monetary Orthodoxy Fails After the Pain Changes
**When to use**: When a society swings from fearing inflation to fearing depression, or the reverse.
**How**: Identify the last major trauma and treat it as the hidden author of current doctrine.
**Trade-offs**: Explains policy mood shifts well; can understate genuine technical improvement.

## Bank Innovation Followed by Rescue Demands
**When to use**: When credit expansion is treated as benign until losses appear.
**How**: Track how private money creation boosts trade or speculation first, then pushes risk onto the public once confidence weakens.
**Trade-offs**: Good for reading crises; can miss cases where regulation actually constrains the cycle.

## Gold Standard Prestige Masks Deflationary Cost
**When to use**: When hard-money discipline is praised as inherently virtuous.
**How**: Ask who bears the unemployment, debt burden, and lost policy flexibility required to keep convertibility intact.
**Trade-offs**: Clarifies political cost; should still acknowledge gold's role in anchoring expectations.

## War Makes Monetary Improvisation Legitimate
**When to use**: When governments suddenly accept paper finance or managed money once emergency arrives.
**How**: Look for moments when fiscal need overrides peacetime doctrine and then permanently changes what later seems possible.
**Trade-offs**: Strong explanatory power; does not mean wartime finance is automatically prudent.

## Crises Educate Institutions
**When to use**: When a reform appears obvious only after collapse.
**How**: Read reforms as products of failure: what could not be defended after panic, depression, or inflation?
**Trade-offs**: Explains institutional learning; also shows why reform usually arrives late.

## Central Bank Prestige Can Hide Plain Error
**When to use**: When official language and institutional dignity make critique seem naive.
**How**: Strip away technical rhetoric and ask whether the institution preserved liquidity, employment, and system stability in practice.
**Trade-offs**: Useful corrective; avoid turning skepticism into blanket dismissal.

## Reserve Currency Success Creates Later Fragility
**When to use**: When one national currency dominates international settlement.
**How**: Track how global demand for that currency supports power at first, then creates convertibility stress, external claims, and confidence problems.
**Trade-offs**: Strong for Bretton Woods and the dollar; can oversimplify multi-currency systems.

## Monetary Policy Is Also Class Politics
**When to use**: When inflation or restraint is presented as neutral expertise.
**How**: Identify who gains from easier credit, who loses from inflation, who benefits from deflation, and how policy distributes adjustment costs.
**Trade-offs**: Adds realism; should not erase real macroeconomic constraints.
