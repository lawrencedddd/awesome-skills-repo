# Cheatsheet

## Quick Reading Rules

| If you see this | Read it as this |
|---|---|
| Hard-money moral language after inflation | A political response to recent monetary trauma |
| Praise for gold as pure stability | A claim that may ignore unemployment and debt costs |
| Rapid credit expansion by banks | New money creation plus delayed fragility |
| Government paper issued in war or emergency | Orthodoxy yielding to necessity |
| Reverence for central bank technique | Check actual crisis performance before accepting the prestige |
| A reserve currency running global deficits | Future pressure on convertibility and confidence |

## Galbraith's Defaults

1. **Start with institutions** - Ask who creates money, under what authority, and with what constraints.
2. **Start with confidence** - Money works because people accept claims as payment.
3. **Distrust monetary piety** - Virtue talk around gold, sound money, or balance often masks distributional choices.
4. **Expect delayed learning** - Reform usually follows collapse, not foresight.
5. **Read policy politically** - Inflation and deflation reallocate wealth and power.

## Gold Standard Test

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Does convertibility constrain employment policy? | If yes, the "discipline" is not free |
| Who absorbs adjustment pressure? | Debtors, workers, and weaker economies often do |
| Is the standard being defended as prestige rather than utility? | Symbolic attachment often outlives practical value |

## Crisis Test

| Ask | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Was the problem one of confidence, liquidity, or solvency? | Distinguish panic from structural failure |
| Which money creator failed: mint, treasury, or bank? | Localize the institutional weakness |
| What old doctrine became untenable? | Find the real lesson of the crisis |

## Keynes Pivot

| Before Keynes | After Keynes |
|---|---|
| Wait for self-correction | Manage demand actively |
| Defend orthodoxy despite unemployment | Accept intervention to prevent prolonged slump |
| Treat money as mainly a standard | Treat money and credit as tools of macro stability |
