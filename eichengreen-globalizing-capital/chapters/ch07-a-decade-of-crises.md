# Chapter 7: A Decade of Crises

## Core Idea
The subprime shock and its aftermath reveal how deeply integrated and still incomplete the contemporary monetary order is. Financial crisis, sovereign stress, euro tensions, currency conflict, and China's rise all become part of one system story.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Crisis cascade in integrated finance**:
  - When to use: when a local shock becomes global.
  - How: follow balance sheets, capital flows, and reserve-currency channels across borders.
- **Incomplete monetary union problem**:
  - When to use: when members share a currency without full fiscal or political union.
  - How: test how adjustment, banking support, and exit politics are handled.

## Key Concepts
- Subprime to GFC
- Greece and Grexit
- Currency wars and China

## Mental Models
- Deep financial integration transmits both liquidity and distress.
- A common currency without full shared backstops can create severe adjustment dilemmas.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating the crisis as purely national rather than systemic.
- Assuming reserve-currency centrality automatically equals control.

## Worked Example
Eichengreen ties together the global financial crisis, euro-area stress, debates over currency manipulation, China's systemic role, and the possibility of digital monetary futures.

## Key Takeaways
1. Modern crises are network events, not isolated failures.
2. The euro exposed the limits of partial monetary union.
3. The international system remains open-ended and contested.
