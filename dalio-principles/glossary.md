# Glossary

**Believability weighting** - Weighting opinions by demonstrated judgment, experience, and ability to explain cause-effect logic rather than giving every opinion equal force. (Ch 5, Ch 11)

**Diagnosis** - Identifying the root causes behind a problem before choosing a fix. (Ch 4, Ch 14)

**Idea meritocracy** - An environment where the best ideas should win through evidence, open debate, and credible judgment rather than hierarchy alone. (Ch 9, Ch 10, Ch 16)

**Machine** - Dalio's model for an organization or personal workflow as a system that predictably produces outcomes. (Ch 13)

**Meaningful relationships** - Trusting, high-honesty relationships strong enough to support direct feedback and shared standards. (Ch 10)

**Pain + Reflection = Progress** - A rule that emotional pain becomes useful when it triggers diagnosis, learning, and principle formation. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**Principles** - Explicit decision rules derived from repeated encounters with reality. (Ch 1)

**Radical open-mindedness** - The discipline of seeking contrary evidence and strong criticism in order to improve judgment. (Ch 5)

**Radical transparency** - Making information, reasoning, and mistakes visible enough that the organization can learn and coordinate around reality. (Ch 10)

**Radical truth** - Speaking and confronting facts plainly instead of softening them for comfort. (Ch 10)

**Responsible Party** - The person explicitly accountable for an outcome, decision, or process. (Ch 15)

**Sorting** - Moving people into roles, support paths, or exits based on actual fit and performance rather than wishful thinking. (Ch 12)

**Taste the soup** - A management habit of directly checking the quality of outputs instead of assuming the system is working. (Ch 14)

**Thoughtful disagreement** - High-quality disagreement aimed at discovering what is true rather than scoring status points. (Ch 5, Ch 11)

**5-Step Process** - Goal setting, problem identification, diagnosis, design, and execution as a closed improvement loop. (Ch 4)
