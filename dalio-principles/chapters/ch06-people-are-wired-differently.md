# Chapter 6: Understand That People Are Wired Differently

## Core Idea
Dalio treats human differences as structural realities. Better management comes from seeing how people are built and matching roles, expectations, and collaboration styles accordingly.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **People are wired very differently**:
  - When to use: when judging performance, communication, or team friction.
  - How: separate moral judgment from design differences.
- **Match strengths to roles**:
  - When to use: staffing, hiring, and coaching.
  - How: identify what the role truly requires, then test fit honestly.

## Key Concepts
- **Strengths and weaknesses package**: talents usually come bundled with limitations.
- **Fit**: performance depends heavily on alignment between person and role.
- **Tolerance**: some weaknesses can be managed; some are role-breaking.

## Mental Models
- Think of people as configurations, not blank slates.
- Use role design to amplify strengths and protect against predictable weaknesses.

## Anti-patterns
- **One-size-fits-all management**: assuming everyone should succeed the same way.
- **Wishful coaching**: treating a mismatch as if effort alone will erase it.

## Worked Example
This section supports many later work principles: role fit, believable judgment, hiring discipline, and sorting all depend on the assumption that people differ in meaningful, persistent ways.

## Key Takeaways
1. Honest assessment is kinder than vague optimism.
2. Fit matters as much as motivation.
3. Teams improve when differences are mapped and managed explicitly.
