# Chapter 7: Learn How to Make Decisions Effectively

## Core Idea
Dalio's decision model combines explicit principles, probabilistic thinking, and second-order judgment. The point is not to eliminate uncertainty but to decide better under it.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Use principles as decision rules**:
  - When to use: recurring judgments under time pressure.
  - How: rely on tested rules rather than improvised reactions.
- **Probabilistic thinking**:
  - When to use: whenever certainty is unavailable.
  - How: weigh odds, scenarios, and asymmetry instead of looking for guaranteed answers.
- **Level-shifting**:
  - When to use: when details are overwhelming.
  - How: move between high-level patterns and low-level facts deliberately.

## Key Concepts
- **Second-order consequences**: the next effects after the obvious first one.
- **Rate vs. level**: not just where something is, but how it is changing.
- **Algorithmic reasoning**: turning strong recurring judgments into explicit rules.

## Mental Models
- Think in bets, not certainties.
- Ask which principle should govern this decision before debating tactics.

## Anti-patterns
- **Narrative capture**: choosing the best story instead of the best logic.
- **Detail drowning**: losing the governing pattern inside facts.

## Worked Example
This chapter ties Dalio's investor mindset to general life decisions: use explicit logic, respect probabilities, and avoid pretending that ambiguity has disappeared.

## Key Takeaways
1. Good decisions come from clear rules plus good evidence.
2. Move between abstract pattern and concrete detail on purpose.
3. Treat judgment as something you can refine and encode.
