# Chapter 15: Design Improvements and Push Through

## Core Idea
Once a problem is diagnosed, the work is to redesign the machine and then execute hard enough for the new design to become real. Dalio ties strategy and discipline tightly together.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Design improvements to your machine**:
  - When to use: after diagnosis.
  - How: change process, roles, incentives, tools, or decision rights so the problem is less likely to recur.
- **Do what you set out to do**:
  - When to use: when a redesign exists but results are not changing.
  - How: assign responsibility, follow through, and hold the line until implementation lands.

## Key Concepts
- **Design over patching**: aim for structural fixes, not temporary relief.
- **Execution discipline**: many good designs die in weak follow-through.
- **Accountability**: someone must own each major step.

## Mental Models
- If a problem keeps recurring, the design is still wrong.
- Good intentions are not evidence of implementation.

## Anti-patterns
- **Patch mentality**: solving today's symptom and preserving tomorrow's failure.
- **Design without follow-through**: analysis that never changes behavior.

## Key Takeaways
1. Fixes should target recurrence, not optics.
2. Execution is a design problem too.
