# Cheatsheet

## Decision Rules

| Situation | Do this | Why |
|---|---|---|
| You hit an unexpected bad outcome | Run the 5-Step Process from the top | Fixing symptoms without diagnosis repeats the failure |
| Two smart people disagree | Use thoughtful disagreement, then believability weighting | Conflict is useful when it improves truth-finding |
| You feel pain, embarrassment, or frustration | Reflect before reacting | Pain is signal, not just something to escape |
| A person keeps struggling in a role | Reassess wiring, fit, and support | Performance problems are often fit or design problems |
| Team quality is drifting | Taste the soup and inspect the machine | Metrics and direct inspection catch decay early |
| A leader becomes a bottleneck | Add tools, protocols, and clearer decision rights | Good systems should not depend on one person |

## 5-Step Process

1. **Goal** - Be specific about what success looks like.
2. **Problem** - Name the obstacle precisely.
3. **Diagnosis** - Find the root cause.
4. **Design** - Change the machine.
5. **Do it** - Push through to the result.

## Believability Weighting Quick Test

| Question | Strong signal |
|---|---|
| Has this person been right about this kind of issue before? | Repeated evidence, not confidence alone |
| Can they explain cause and effect? | Clear reasoning, not slogans |
| Do they engage criticism well? | Updates view under pressure |
| Do they understand the details and the higher level? | Can move between facts and synthesis |

## Tells and Smells

| If you see this | Suspect this |
|---|---|
| Fast solutioning with vague problem statements | Diagnosis is being skipped |
| Harmony at all costs | Truth is being suppressed |
| Repeated surprises from the same area | The machine lacks good metrics or oversight |
| Great intentions with weak results | Role fit, incentives, or process design is off |
| Heavy dependence on one heroic manager | Governance and institutionalization are weak |

## Defaults

- Default to evidence over seniority.
- Default to explicit principles over ad hoc judgment.
- Default to surfacing problems early.
- Default to role fit over wishful coaching.
- Default to changing the machine, not just blaming the person.
