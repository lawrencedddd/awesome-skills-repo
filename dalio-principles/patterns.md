# Patterns

## Convert Pain Into a Principle
**When to use**: After a mistake, conflict, or disappointing result.
**How**: Write down what happened, identify the cause, state the lesson as a reusable rule, and apply it the next time a similar pattern appears.
**Trade-offs**: Good for long-term learning; bad if used as an excuse to over-theorize and delay action.

## Run the 5-Step Loop
**When to use**: Any recurring goal where results matter.
**How**: Define the goal clearly, identify the obstacle, diagnose the cause, redesign the approach, and execute until the result changes.
**Trade-offs**: Structured and reliable; can feel slow if a team wants to jump straight to solutions.

## Believability-Weighted Debate
**When to use**: Important decisions with disagreement.
**How**: Gather opposing views, identify who has a strong track record on the specific issue, require cause-effect reasoning, then weight the final call accordingly.
**Trade-offs**: Produces better decisions; requires a culture that can tolerate direct challenge.

## Match Role to Wiring
**When to use**: Hiring, staffing, coaching, and succession planning.
**How**: Identify the role's real demands, assess the person's strengths and weaknesses, and decide whether to train, redesign the role, or change the assignment.
**Trade-offs**: Improves fit and accountability; can feel blunt if the culture avoids clear judgments.

## Treat the Organization as a Machine
**When to use**: Output quality, coordination, or scale problems.
**How**: Inspect process design, incentives, reporting lines, metrics, and people fit instead of blaming isolated episodes.
**Trade-offs**: Good for repeatability; can become mechanical if leaders forget the human side.

## Surface Problems Early
**When to use**: Quality-sensitive work where small defects compound.
**How**: Reward people for flagging issues, monitor outputs directly, and make unresolved problems visible.
**Trade-offs**: Prevents drift; increases friction for teams that prefer harmony over clarity.

## Diagnose Before Fixing
**When to use**: When the same issue reappears or a fast fix did not hold.
**How**: Separate symptoms from causes, identify whether the problem is with design or execution, and verify the diagnosis with evidence.
**Trade-offs**: Avoids wasted effort; demands patience and analytical discipline.

## Institutionalize With Tools and Protocols
**When to use**: When good behavior depends too much on memory or one leader.
**How**: Encode expectations into checklists, meeting rules, metrics, software, and decision logs.
**Trade-offs**: Scales quality; too much tooling can turn judgment into bureaucracy.

## Protect the System With Governance
**When to use**: Leadership transitions, high-stakes authority, or rapid growth.
**How**: Clarify oversight, reporting lines, decision rights, and escalation mechanisms so no single person outruns the system.
**Trade-offs**: Reduces key-person risk; adds process and can slow unilateral action.
