## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### Start with reality, not preference
- **Embrace Reality and Deal with It**: Start from what is true, even when it is unpleasant, and then choose what to do. Dalio treats truth as leverage: seeing reality clearly raises the odds of good decisions.
- **Pain + Reflection = Progress**: Pain is useful when it points to a lesson. Reflection turns bad outcomes into principles you can reuse instead of repeating the same mistakes.
- **Evolve or die**: People and organizations are always being tested by reality. Improvement is not optional; it is the price of continued relevance.

### Use an explicit loop for progress
- **The 5-Step Process**: 1) set clear goals, 2) identify and refuse to tolerate problems, 3) diagnose root causes, 4) design improvements, 5) push through to results.
- Treat each step as distinct. People commonly rush from a bad outcome to action and skip diagnosis. Dalio is explicit that this weakens the whole loop.
- **Think of yourself as both worker and designer**: do the work, but also step above it and redesign the system producing the results.

### Improve thinking quality through openness
- **Radical Open-Mindedness**: Seek the strongest objections to your view, especially from credible people with a track record of being right about the same kind of problem.
- **Thoughtful Disagreement**: Productive conflict is a feature, not a bug, when both parties are trying to get at what is true.
- **Believability Weighting**: Not all opinions should count equally. Weight views by demonstrated judgment, experience, and ability to explain cause-effect logic.

### Account for human differences
- **People are wired very differently**: Many conflicts are not moral failures but mismatches in thinking style, temperament, and capabilities.
- Manage people by understanding their design, not by assuming everyone can excel at everything.
- Good role design matches responsibilities to strengths and tolerable weaknesses.

### Build organizations as idea meritocracies
- **Radical Truth + Radical Transparency**: Accurate shared understanding gets easier when mistakes, disagreements, and evidence are discussable in the open.
- **Meaningful Work and Meaningful Relationships**: High standards work better when paired with trust, mutual care, and shared mission.
- **Get and Stay in Sync**: Surface disagreements early, clarify decision rights, and keep people aligned on goals, facts, and tradeoffs.

### Run the organization like a machine
- **The Machine**: Every organization is a system producing outcomes. If outcomes are weak, inspect people, design, incentives, metrics, and workflows.
- **Perceive and don't tolerate problems**: Problems are signals. Reward finding them early.
- **Diagnose root causes**: Be specific about whether a failure came from design, execution, capability, or incentives.
- **Design improvements**: Change the machine so the same failure is less likely next time.
- **Use tools and protocols**: Good processes should not depend on memory, charisma, or one person's judgment.

### Governance matters
- **Checks and balances**: Even strong cultures need explicit governance. Clear decision rights, oversight, and escalation paths reduce key-person risk and power drift.
- A great partnership and a sound system are complementary. Governance cannot rescue weak character, but weak governance eventually punishes strong cultures too.
