# Patterns

## Supply-demand three-step method
**When to use**: Market news, policy changes, cost shocks, or demand shocks.
**How**: Identify whether demand, supply, or both move; determine direction; compare old and new equilibrium.
**Trade-offs**: Clear but static; long-run adjustment may require new elasticity and entry assumptions.

## Elasticity-revenue test
**When to use**: Price changes, quantity restrictions, taxes, bans, and revenue questions.
**How**: If demand is inelastic, price increases raise total revenue; if elastic, price increases reduce total revenue.
**Trade-offs**: Short-run and long-run elasticities may differ.

## Tax incidence analysis
**When to use**: Figuring out who really bears a tax.
**How**: Draw the tax wedge, compare elasticities, and assign more burden to the less elastic side.
**Trade-offs**: Legal remittance is not economic burden.

## Surplus-efficiency analysis
**When to use**: Taxes, tariffs, price controls, monopoly, and trade policy.
**How**: Track consumer surplus, producer surplus, government revenue, and deadweight loss separately.
**Trade-offs**: Efficiency analysis does not settle fairness.

## Market-failure diagnosis
**When to use**: Deciding whether intervention might improve outcomes.
**How**: Check externalities, public goods, common resources, market power, and asymmetric information.
**Trade-offs**: Finding market failure is not enough; compare policy failure and administrative costs.

## Firm marginal decision rule
**When to use**: Output, shutdown, entry, or exit decisions.
**How**: Produce where MR=MC; for competitive firms, continue short run if P>=AVC and remain long run if P>=ATC.
**Trade-offs**: Market structure determines whether MR equals price.

## Asymmetric-information diagnosis
**When to use**: Insurance, credit, hiring, used goods, platform governance.
**How**: Hidden action means moral hazard; hidden characteristics mean adverse selection; respond with monitoring, incentives, signaling, or screening.
**Trade-offs**: Mechanisms have costs and can create new distortions.
