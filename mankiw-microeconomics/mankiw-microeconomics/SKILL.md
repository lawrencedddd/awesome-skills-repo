## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Scarcity -> trade-offs -> opportunity cost**: Start by asking what is scarce and what the best forgone alternative is.
- **Marginal analysis**: Act when marginal benefit exceeds marginal cost; stop when it does not.
- **Incentives matter**: Policies and institutions change payoffs, so behavior changes.
- **Comparative advantage**: Specialize by lower opportunity cost, not absolute productivity.
- **Supply-demand equilibrium**: For any shock, identify affected curve, movement direction, and new equilibrium.
- **Elasticity**: Responsiveness determines revenue effects, tax burden, and deadweight loss.
- **Tax incidence**: The less elastic side bears more burden, regardless of who legally pays.
- **Surplus and efficiency**: Consumer surplus plus producer surplus measures gains from trade.
- **Deadweight loss**: Taxes, monopoly, tariffs, and binding price controls destroy surplus by preventing mutually beneficial trades.
- **Market failure diagnosis**: Check externalities, public goods, common resources, market power, and asymmetric information before recommending intervention.
- **Firm decision rules**: Competitive firms produce where P = MC, shut down short run if P < AVC, and exit long run if P < ATC.
- **Game theory**: In small-number markets, Nash equilibrium may be worse than cooperation because each player has a private incentive to defect.
- **Factor markets**: Labor demand is derived from output demand; hire until VMPL = wage.
- **Consumer choice**: Budget constraints plus preferences generate demand; price changes split into substitution and income effects.
- **Information economics**: Hidden action creates moral hazard; hidden characteristics create adverse selection; use monitoring, incentives, signaling, and screening.
