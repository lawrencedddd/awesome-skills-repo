# Glossary

**Activity rule** - restriction that prevents a bidder from increasing total bidding activity in later rounds. (Ch 6)

**Approximate matroid** - matroid-like inner approximation of a hard feasible set, used to justify greedy algorithms. (Ch 4)

**Assignment constraint** - rule governing which exact item, time, channel, or location can be assigned to which participant. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 6)

**Assignment round** - post-auction round in which winners bid for exact frequencies or other detailed assignments. (Ch 6)

**Bidder submodularity** - condition under which a bidder's marginal contribution decreases as the set of other bidders grows. (Ch 3)

**Clock auction** - dynamic auction in which a displayed price changes and bidders respond over rounds. (Ch 4, Ch 6)

**Coase theorem** - proposition that, under ideal property-right and bargaining assumptions, parties can bargain to efficient outcomes regardless of initial ownership. (Ch 1)

**Complex constraint** - feasibility condition that depends on combinations, assignments, or compatibility rather than aggregate quantity alone. (Ch 1)

**Conditional reserve** - FCC rule that reserves some licenses for qualified bidders after a revenue threshold is met. (Ch 6)

**Core** - set of payoff vectors not blocked by any coalition that could do better on its own. (Ch 3)

**Deferred-acceptance clock auction** - descending clock auction in which sellers are accepted only at the end and feasibility is maintained through exit rules. (Ch 4)

**Essential bidder** - bidder whose exit would make the target infeasible; its price stops falling. (Ch 4)

**Final-stage rule** - FCC rule determining whether auction revenue is enough to close at the current clearing target. (Ch 6)

**Forward auction** - auction in which the FCC sells newly available broadband spectrum licenses. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Graph coloring** - assignment problem used to model TV channel feasibility under interference constraints. (Ch 1, Ch 4)

**Greedy algorithm** - algorithm that processes options in priority order and accepts each if feasible. (Ch 2, Ch 4)

**Gross substitutes** - condition where raising one good's price does not reduce demand for other goods. (Ch 2)

**Impairment** - limitation on broadband license usability caused by remaining interference. (Ch 6)

**Intra-round bidding** - bidding that allows demand changes at prices inside a clock round's interval. (Ch 6)

**Knapsack problem** - selection problem of maximizing value subject to a capacity constraint. (Ch 2)

**Market-clearing price** - price at which supply equals demand. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Monotone winner selection** - allocation rule where improving a winning bid cannot turn it into a losing bid. (Ch 2)

**Near-substitutes** - goods or constraints that are not exact substitutes but are close enough for monotone approximations to perform well. (Ch 2, Ch 4)

**Obvious strategy-proofness** - incentive property where truthfulness is evident from local best-case/worst-case comparisons. (Ch 4)

**Partial Economic Area (PEA)** - geographic area used as a license unit in the FCC forward auction. (Ch 6)

**Pseudo-equilibrium** - approximate market-clearing condition for indivisible or hard allocation problems. (Ch 2)

**Reverse auction** - auction in which the FCC buys rights from broadcasters. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Stable matching** - matching with no blocking pair and no participant wishing to reject its assignment. (Ch 2)

**Strategy-proofness** - property that truthful reporting or truthful play is optimal regardless of others' actions. (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Substitutability index** - measure of how well a matroid approximation captures a feasible set and bounds greedy performance. (Ch 4)

**Threshold auction** - strategy-proof auction where each winner pays the bid threshold at which it would stop winning. (Ch 2)

**Vickrey auction** - efficient strategy-proof mechanism using externality payments. (Ch 3)
