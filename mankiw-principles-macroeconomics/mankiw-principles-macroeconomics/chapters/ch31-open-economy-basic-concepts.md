# Chapter 31: Open-Economy Macroeconomics: Basic Concepts

## Core Idea
Open economies trade goods, services, and financial assets; net exports equal net capital outflow by accounting identity.

## Mental Models
- Separate **real variables** from **nominal variables** before explaining causality.
- Distinguish **accounting identities** from behavioral theories.
- Ask whether the question is about the **long run** or the **short run**; Mankiw's answer often changes with the time horizon.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating GDP as welfare**: GDP is useful but omits leisure, inequality, nonmarket production, and environmental quality.
- **Ignoring expectations**: inflation, interest rates, and policy effects depend on what people expected before the policy.
- **Mixing identities with policy claims**: an identity must hold, but it does not by itself say what caused what.

## Worked Example
If a country buys more foreign goods than it sells, foreigners must be acquiring domestic assets or claims: a trade deficit is matched by capital inflow.

## Key Takeaways
1. Trade balances and capital flows are two sides of the same accounting coin.
2. Real exchange rates determine relative competitiveness.
3. Purchasing-power parity is a useful long-run benchmark but weak short-run predictor.
