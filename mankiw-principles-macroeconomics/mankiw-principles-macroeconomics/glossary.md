# Glossary

**aggregate-demand curve**: Relationship between the price level and quantity of aggregate output demanded. (Ch 33)
**aggregate-supply curve**: Relationship between the price level and quantity of aggregate output supplied. (Ch 33)
**automatic stabilizers**: Fiscal mechanisms that stimulate or restrain demand without new legislation. (Ch 34)
**central bank**: Institution that oversees the banking system and regulates the money supply. (Ch 29)
**consumer price index**: Measure of the overall cost of goods and services bought by a typical consumer. (Ch 24)
**crowding out**: Reduction in investment caused by government borrowing or fiscal expansion raising interest rates. (Ch 26, Ch 34)
**cyclical unemployment**: Deviation of unemployment from its natural rate. (Ch 28)
**GDP deflator**: Price index calculated from nominal GDP and real GDP. (Ch 23)
**gross domestic product**: Market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a period. (Ch 23)
**inflation rate**: Percentage change in a price index. (Ch 24, Ch 30)
**loanable funds**: Income that people choose to save and lend rather than consume. (Ch 26)
**money multiplier**: Amount of money the banking system generates from each unit of reserves. (Ch 29)
**national saving**: Total income remaining after consumption and government purchases. (Ch 26)
**natural rate of unemployment**: Normal unemployment rate around which actual unemployment fluctuates. (Ch 28, Ch 35)
**net capital outflow**: Purchase of foreign assets by domestic residents minus purchase of domestic assets by foreigners. (Ch 31)
**net exports**: Exports minus imports. (Ch 23, Ch 31)
**nominal exchange rate**: Rate at which one currency trades for another. (Ch 31)
**Phillips curve**: Short-run relationship between inflation and unemployment. (Ch 35)
**present value**: Amount today needed to produce a future amount at a given interest rate. (Ch 27)
**quantity equation**: M x V = P x Y. (Ch 30)
**real exchange rate**: Rate at which goods and services of one country trade for those of another. (Ch 31)
**real GDP**: Production valued at constant prices. (Ch 23)
**real interest rate**: Nominal interest rate minus inflation. (Ch 24)
**recession**: Period of falling real incomes and rising unemployment. (Ch 33)
**stagflation**: Falling output with rising prices, often after adverse supply shocks. (Ch 33)
**velocity of money**: Rate at which money changes hands. (Ch 30)
