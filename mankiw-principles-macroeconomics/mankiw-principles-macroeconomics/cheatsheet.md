# Macroeconomics Cheatsheet

## Core Identities and Formulas

| Concept | Formula / Rule |
|---|---|
| GDP expenditure | Y = C + I + G + NX |
| GDP deflator | 100 x nominal GDP / real GDP |
| Inflation rate | percentage change in price index |
| Real interest rate | nominal interest rate - inflation rate |
| National saving | S = Y - C - G |
| Private saving | Y - T - C |
| Public saving | T - G |
| Closed economy | S = I |
| Open economy | S = I + NCO |
| Net exports identity | NX = NCO |
| Quantity equation | M x V = P x Y |
| Unemployment rate | unemployed / labor force x 100 |
| Labor-force participation | labor force / adult population x 100 |

## Decision Rules

| Question | Start Here | Watch For |
|---|---|---|
| Is living standard rising? | Real GDP per person and productivity | GDP misses leisure, distribution, environment |
| Are prices rising? | CPI and GDP deflator | Substitution bias and quality change |
| Why is growth faster? | Capital, human capital, technology, institutions | Diminishing returns and catch-up |
| What does a deficit do? | Loanable-funds model | Crowding out and capital inflows |
| What does money growth do? | Quantity equation | Long-run neutrality versus short-run demand effects |
| Why unemployment persists? | Search, minimum wage, unions, efficiency wages | Natural versus cyclical unemployment |
| What does a tariff do? | Open-economy model | NX may not improve because exchange rates adjust |
| Why recession? | AD-AS shock diagnosis | Demand shocks lower output and prices; supply shocks lower output and raise prices |
| Should policy stabilize? | Lags, multipliers, crowding out, expectations | Policy may arrive late or shift expectations |
| Can inflation be lowered? | Phillips curve and credibility | Sacrifice ratio depends on expectations |

## Policy Triage

1. Define the target variable: output, employment, inflation, exchange rate, debt, or growth.
2. Identify the time horizon: short-run stabilization or long-run living standards.
3. Write the relevant identity or model.
4. Locate the shock or policy instrument.
5. Trace direct effects, equilibrium feedback, and expectations.
6. Separate efficiency, distribution, and political feasibility.
