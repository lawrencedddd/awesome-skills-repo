## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **GDP Accounting**: use `Y = C + I + G + NX` to decompose expenditure and distinguish nominal GDP, real GDP, and the GDP deflator.
- **Cost-of-Living Measurement**: use CPI to convert nominal values into real values, while remembering substitution bias, new goods, and quality-change limits.
- **Productivity Theory of Living Standards**: long-run prosperity depends on output per worker, driven by physical capital, human capital, natural resources, and technological knowledge.
- **Loanable Funds Model**: saving supplies loanable funds, investment demands them, and the real interest rate equilibrates the market.
- **Financial Present Value**: discount future payments and account for risk before comparing financial choices.
- **Natural Rate of Unemployment**: distinguish frictional and structural unemployment from cyclical unemployment.
- **Money and Banking**: money serves as medium of exchange, unit of account, and store of value; fractional-reserve banking creates deposits.
- **Quantity Theory of Money**: `M x V = P x Y`; in the long run, sustained money growth mainly raises prices.
- **Open-Economy Identities**: net exports equal net capital outflow, and saving equals domestic investment plus net capital outflow.
- **Open-Economy Two-Market Model**: loanable funds determine the real interest rate; foreign-currency exchange determines the real exchange rate, with net capital outflow linking them.
- **Aggregate Demand and Aggregate Supply**: use AD-AS to analyze short-run fluctuations in output and the price level.
- **Liquidity Preference and Stabilization Policy**: monetary and fiscal policy shift aggregate demand through interest rates, multipliers, crowding out, and automatic stabilizers.
- **Phillips Curve**: policymakers face a short-run inflation-unemployment trade-off but not a stable long-run trade-off.
- **Policy Debate Framework**: evaluate macro policy through lags, credibility, expectations, debt sustainability, and incentive effects.
