# Patterns

## Identity First
**When to use**: GDP, saving-investment, open-economy, and monetary questions.
**How**: Write the relevant identity before making causal claims.
**Trade-offs**: Identities constrain outcomes but do not identify causation.

## Real Versus Nominal Split
**When to use**: GDP, wages, interest rates, exchange rates, money, inflation.
**How**: Deflate nominal values by a price index or compare with inflation.
**Trade-offs**: Real measures are better for purchasing power; nominal measures matter for contracts and accounting.

## Long Run Versus Short Run
**When to use**: Money, output, unemployment, policy stabilization.
**How**: In the long run, real variables depend on real factors; in the short run, aggregate demand can move output away from natural output.
**Trade-offs**: The right model depends on time horizon.

## Loanable-Funds Shock Analysis
**When to use**: Saving, investment, deficits, tax incentives.
**How**: Determine whether saving supply or investment demand shifts; infer real interest rate and investment effects.
**Trade-offs**: Closed-economy model abstracts from capital flows.

## Money-Supply Transmission
**When to use**: Central bank policy and bank lending.
**How**: Trace reserves, deposits, money multiplier, interest rates, aggregate demand, and price-level effects.
**Trade-offs**: Banking behavior and reserve policy can weaken simple multiplier predictions.

## Open-Economy Two-Market Analysis
**When to use**: trade deficits, tariffs, budget deficits, capital flight, exchange rates.
**How**: Use loanable funds, net capital outflow, and foreign-currency exchange together.
**Trade-offs**: Accounting identities hold exactly; model predictions depend on behavioral responses.

## AD-AS Shock Diagnosis
**When to use**: recessions, inflation, stagflation, stabilization policy.
**How**: Identify demand shock versus supply shock; shift AD or SRAS; compare output and price level.
**Trade-offs**: Policy responses can have lags and expectation effects.

## Phillips Curve Policy Test
**When to use**: inflation-unemployment trade-off and disinflation.
**How**: Ask whether expected inflation is fixed or adjusting; distinguish short-run movement from long-run vertical curve.
**Trade-offs**: Credibility changes the cost of disinflation.
