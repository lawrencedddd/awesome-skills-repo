# Chapter 11: Security Analysis for the Lay Investor

    ## Core Idea
    Security analysis for ordinary investors should focus on a few durable questions: earnings power, financial strength, asset backing, dividend record, prospects, and price paid.

    ## Frameworks Introduced
    - **Five-factor common-stock analysis**: A Graham-style decision tool for this chapter.
  - When to use: When judging whether a security, policy, or market claim deserves investor capital.
  - How: Start with protection of principal, normalize the evidence, compare price with value, and require a reasoned margin before acting.

    ## Key Concepts
    - **Intrinsic value**: A conservative estimate of business value based on facts rather than market mood.
    - **Margin of safety**: The discount or protection that absorbs analytical error and adverse developments.
    - **Earnings power**: Normalized ability of a business to generate profits through cycles.
    - **Financial strength**: Balance-sheet and coverage protection against adversity.
    - **Temperament**: The investor's ability to follow policy despite market emotion.

    ## Mental Models
    - Use **businesslike investing** when deciding whether you are buying an ownership interest or a price movement.
    - Use **price-paid discipline** because future return is shaped by the price you pay today.
    - Use **avoidance as skill** when a category or security requires too many favorable assumptions.

    ## Anti-patterns
    - **Double-counting good management**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.
- **Paying for distant prospects as certainty**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.
- **Ignoring capital structure**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.

    ## Worked Example
    A company with the same earnings as another but less debt, more liquid assets, and a steadier record deserves more confidence. Graham's analysis begins with protection before projection.

    ## Key Takeaways
    1. Analyze what can be known before guessing what may happen.
2. Financial strength is part of value.
3. Prospects matter, but price and evidence matter more.
