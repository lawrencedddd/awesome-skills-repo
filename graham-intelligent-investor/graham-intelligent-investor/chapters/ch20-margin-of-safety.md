# Chapter 20: Margin of Safety as the Central Concept of Investment

    ## Core Idea
    Margin of safety is Graham's central principle: buy only when price is sufficiently below conservatively estimated value to absorb errors, bad luck, and uncertainty.

    ## Frameworks Introduced
    - **Margin of safety**: A Graham-style decision tool for this chapter.
  - When to use: When judging whether a security, policy, or market claim deserves investor capital.
  - How: Start with protection of principal, normalize the evidence, compare price with value, and require a reasoned margin before acting.

    ## Key Concepts
    - **Intrinsic value**: A conservative estimate of business value based on facts rather than market mood.
    - **Margin of safety**: The discount or protection that absorbs analytical error and adverse developments.
    - **Earnings power**: Normalized ability of a business to generate profits through cycles.
    - **Financial strength**: Balance-sheet and coverage protection against adversity.
    - **Temperament**: The investor's ability to follow policy despite market emotion.

    ## Mental Models
    - Use **businesslike investing** when deciding whether you are buying an ownership interest or a price movement.
    - Use **price-paid discipline** because future return is shaped by the price you pay today.
    - Use **avoidance as skill** when a category or security requires too many favorable assumptions.

    ## Anti-patterns
    - **Paying full price for perfection**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.
- **Depending on forecasts to be exactly right**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.
- **Confusing risk with price volatility alone**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.

    ## Worked Example
    A bond investor demands coverage above interest requirements; a stock investor demands a purchase price low enough relative to earnings power and assets that mistakes need not be fatal. The same principle governs both.

    ## Key Takeaways
    1. The future is uncertain; price must compensate.
2. Margin of safety converts analysis into protection.
3. Investment is most intelligent when most businesslike.
