# Chapter 6: Portfolio Policy for the Enterprising Investor: Negative Approach

    ## Core Idea
    The enterprising investor begins by knowing what to avoid: low-quality bonds, preferred stocks with weak protection, foreign-government issues without adequate safeguards, new issues, and fashionable growth purchased at excessive prices.

    ## Key Concepts
    - **Intrinsic value**: A conservative estimate of business value based on facts rather than market mood.
    - **Margin of safety**: The discount or protection that absorbs analytical error and adverse developments.
    - **Earnings power**: Normalized ability of a business to generate profits through cycles.
    - **Financial strength**: Balance-sheet and coverage protection against adversity.
    - **Temperament**: The investor's ability to follow policy despite market emotion.

    ## Mental Models
    - Use **businesslike investing** when deciding whether you are buying an ownership interest or a price movement.
    - Use **price-paid discipline** because future return is shaped by the price you pay today.
    - Use **avoidance as skill** when a category or security requires too many favorable assumptions.

    ## Anti-patterns
    - **Buying IPOs from sales pressure**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.
- **Reaching for yield**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.
- **Equating a good company with a good investment**: It fails because it substitutes hope, fashion, or sales pressure for analysis and safety.

    ## Worked Example
    A new issue is usually sold under conditions favorable to the seller, promoted by optimistic narratives and distribution machinery. Graham's default is avoidance unless the buyer can prove exceptional value independently.

    ## Key Takeaways
    1. Avoidance is an active investment tool.
2. The enterprising investor earns freedom only through discipline.
3. Many losses come from categories that can be ruled out in advance.
