# Intelligent Investor Cheatsheet

## First Gate

| Question | If no | Graham response |
|---|---|---|
| Do I understand the security as a business interest or claim? | No | Do not buy |
| Is there thorough analysis? | No | Speculation, not investment |
| Is principal reasonably protected? | No | Demand lower price or avoid |
| Is expected return adequate? | No | Wait |
| Is there a margin of safety? | No | Price is too high or evidence too weak |

## Investor Type

| If you want | Choose | Required behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Simplicity and satisfactory results | Defensive policy | Diversify, use high-grade bonds, buy quality stocks at moderate prices |
| Potentially better results through work | Enterprising policy | Research bargains, avoid weak categories, diversify special situations |
| Excitement or short-term bets | Speculation account | Separate, small, never confused with investing |

## Defensive Stock Screen

| Test | Graham-style intent |
|---|---|
| Adequate size | Avoid fragile enterprises |
| Strong financial condition | Protect against adversity |
| Earnings stability | Avoid chronic loss-makers |
| Long dividend record | Evidence of durability and shareholder treatment |
| Some earnings growth | Avoid stagnation at any price |
| Moderate P/E | Do not overpay for earnings |
| Moderate price/assets | Keep valuation anchored |

## Red Flags

| Smell | Likely problem |
|---|---|
| A story depends on rapid future growth | Forecast doing too much work |
| EPS looks good but dilution is large | Per-share economics overstated |
| Bond yield is high and coverage is weak | Yield is compensation for danger |
| IPO is heavily promoted | Seller-friendly timing |
| A great company trades at a heroic multiple | Good business, bad investment risk |
| Market price changes your conviction daily | Mr. Market is mastering you |
| Management retains earnings without strong returns | Poor owner stewardship |

## Decision Rules

- When in doubt, demand a wider margin of safety.
- When price is high, future returns are already being consumed.
- When a security requires exact forecasts, it is too fragile for Graham's standard.
- When a category is structurally promotional, avoidance is a valid strategy.
- When market mood is extreme, rebalance toward policy rather than emotion.
- When earnings are abnormal, average and adjust before valuing.
- When advice is offered, inspect incentives before conclusions.
