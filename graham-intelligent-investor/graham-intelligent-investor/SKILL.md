## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### Investment Is Not Speculation
Use Graham's investment test before any purchase: an investment operation must rest on thorough analysis, promise safety of principal, and offer an adequate return. If one of those elements is missing, call it speculation, size it accordingly, and do not pretend that confidence has converted it into investment.

### Margin of Safety Is the Central Concept
Prefer investments where conservative value materially exceeds price, or where interest and asset coverage protect the downside. The point is not to be precisely right; it is to be protected when you are partly wrong. Price is the investor's main defense against uncertainty.

### Mr. Market Is Servant, Not Master
Treat daily market quotations as offers from an emotional business partner. You may buy from him, sell to him, or ignore him. The intelligent investor uses market fluctuations to improve terms rather than letting them dictate beliefs.

### Defensive vs Enterprising Investor
Choose your policy by temperament, time, and competence. The defensive investor emphasizes simplicity, high-grade bonds, diversified common stocks, and strict quality/price filters. The enterprising investor may seek bargain issues, special situations, and neglected securities, but earns that freedom only through greater work and discipline.

### Price Paid Determines Future Return
A wonderful company can be a poor investment at too high a price. Growth, quality, and management matter, but they do not cancel arithmetic. Always translate optimism into the multiple, yield, asset value, and assumptions embedded in the price.

### Safety Before Forecasting
Graham distrusts distant forecasts. Start with balance-sheet strength, normalized earnings, dividend history, coverage ratios, and valuation. Use future prospects as a supplement, not a substitute for evidence.

### Avoidance Is a Positive Skill
The investor improves results by refusing categories with poor odds: overpromoted new issues, low-quality bonds bought for yield, fashionable growth stocks at extreme prices, opaque accounting, weak coverage, and securities whose appeal depends on a perfect future.

### Rules Protect Temperament
Asset-allocation ranges, diversification, valuation limits, and checklists exist because investor emotion is unreliable. Graham's rules are not mechanical magic; they are guardrails against panic, greed, salesmanship, and overconfidence.

### Earnings Must Be Normalized
Do not accept reported per-share earnings at face value. Adjust for dilution, special charges, cyclicality, accounting choices, and the difference between peak earnings and durable earning power.

### Shareholders Are Owners
Stock ownership is business ownership. Management should be judged by capital allocation, dividend policy, treatment of owners, and evidence that retained earnings create value.
