# Patterns

## Investment-vs-Speculation Gate
**When to use**: Before buying any security.
**How**: Ask whether the decision rests on thorough analysis, safety of principal, and adequate return. If not, label it speculation and limit it.
**Trade-offs**: Conservative classification may reject exciting opportunities, but prevents hidden risk-taking.

## Margin-of-Safety Appraisal
**When to use**: Any valuation, bond purchase, stock purchase, or special situation.
**How**: Estimate value conservatively, normalize earnings, inspect balance-sheet protection, and buy only at a discount large enough to absorb error.
**Trade-offs**: Waiting for a margin may leave cash idle during expensive markets.

## Defensive Investor Screen
**When to use**: Building a low-maintenance common-stock portfolio.
**How**: Require adequate size, strong finances, stable earnings, long dividend record, some growth, moderate P/E, and moderate price-to-assets.
**Trade-offs**: Excludes many glamorous companies and may lag speculative markets.

## Enterprising Bargain Search
**When to use**: Investor has time, skill, and discipline for extra analysis.
**How**: Search neglected, statistically cheap, financially sound securities; diversify across many candidates; demand clear downside protection.
**Trade-offs**: Requires patience, accounting work, and tolerance for unpopular holdings.

## Mr. Market Reframing
**When to use**: Volatility creates emotional pressure.
**How**: Treat price as an offer. Accept only if it improves your businesslike position; otherwise ignore it.
**Trade-offs**: Requires independent valuation and emotional discipline.

## Earnings-Quality Audit
**When to use**: Reported EPS drives a valuation.
**How**: Adjust for dilution, special items, cyclicality, accounting changes, tax anomalies, and peak-year effects.
**Trade-offs**: Normalization can be judgment-heavy and may miss genuine improvements.

## New-Issue Default Avoidance
**When to use**: IPOs, promoted offerings, and hot securities.
**How**: Assume the seller picked favorable timing; require independent value evidence before participating.
**Trade-offs**: Avoids many promotions but may miss rare exceptional offerings.

## Adviser Incentive Audit
**When to use**: Receiving recommendations from brokers, advisers, fund marketers, or analysts.
**How**: Identify compensation, conflicts, sales pressure, and whether advice fits your policy.
**Trade-offs**: Skepticism protects capital but requires more self-reliance.

## Shareholder Stewardship Review
**When to use**: Evaluating management and dividend policy.
**How**: Ask whether retained earnings produce adequate returns and whether management treats shareholders as owners.
**Trade-offs**: Owner activism may be difficult for small shareholders, but the lens improves security selection.
