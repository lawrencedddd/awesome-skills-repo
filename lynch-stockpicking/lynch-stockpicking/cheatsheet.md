# Cheatsheet

## First Question

| If you have... | Ask next |
|---|---|
| A great product | Is the company public, and does this product move earnings? |
| A cheap-looking stock | Cheap relative to what: earnings, assets, cash flow, or cycle? |
| A falling stock | Is the business worth more now, or just lower-priced? |
| A rising stock | Has the story improved enough to justify the price? |
| A turnaround | Can it survive without bankruptcy or major dilution? |
| A fast grower | How long can growth continue, and at what P/E? |

## Six Categories

| Category | Buy For | Watch | Sell When |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slow grower | Dividend, modest stability | Debt, acquisition mistakes | 30-50% gain or fundamentals worsen |
| Stalwart | Moderate gain, resilience | P/E too high, growth slowing | Expected gain reached without better story |
| Fast grower | Earnings expansion, tenbagger potential | Balance sheet, runway, P/E vs growth | Growth breaks or valuation outruns story |
| Cyclical | Recovery from trough | Inventories, capacity, cycle peak | Conditions look best and expectations peak |
| Turnaround | Survival plus repair | Cash, debt, dilution | Rescue stalls or balance sheet worsens |
| Asset play | Hidden value | Debt, catalyst, asset quality | Value recognized or asset thesis impaired |

## Lynch Likes

| Signal | Why It Helps |
|---|---|
| Dull name/business | Fewer investors compete for it |
| Disagreeable business | Neglect can create mispricing |
| Spinoff | Institutions may sell mechanically |
| Niche or brand | Protects margins |
| Recurring purchase | Supports steady earnings |
| Insider buying | Management is aligned with owners |
| Share buybacks | Raises per-share claim if done sensibly |
| Low institutional ownership | More room for discovery |

## Lynch Avoids

| Red Flag | Why |
|---|---|
| Hottest stock in hottest industry | Competition and expectations surge |
| "Next X" | Analogy substitutes for analysis |
| Diworseification | Cash wasted outside core competence |
| Whisper stock | Rumor without earnings |
| One-customer dependence | Customer can crush bargaining power |
| Exciting name | Attracts buyers before proof |
| Inventory rising faster than sales | Demand may be weakening |
| Heavy debt in trouble | Creditors can control the outcome |

## Two-Minute Drill Template

```text
This is a [category].
The company makes money by [business].
The story is [earnings driver].
The stock is priced at [P/E or asset value logic].
The balance sheet is [cash/debt].
The key evidence I need to monitor is [facts].
I am wrong if [sell trigger].
```

## Hard Rules

- A product you like is a lead, not a buy signal.
- Follow earnings, not the stock price alone.
- Compare P/E to realistic growth.
- Companies with no debt cannot go bankrupt.
- Do not diversify into companies you do not understand.
- Do not sell just because the stock went up.
- Do not hold just because the stock went down.
- Recheck the story every few months.
