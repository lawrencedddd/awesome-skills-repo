# Glossary

**Central bank** - The institution that comes to coordinate payments, provide emergency liquidity, supervise or influence banks, and support monetary stability. (Ch 1, Ch 7, Ch 8)

**Clearinghouse** - The mechanism or institution through which banks settle claims against each other, often developing monitoring and disciplinary powers. (Ch 3)

**Club** - A private or semi-private arrangement among banks that sets rules for membership, conduct, and support, often foreshadowing central-bank functions. (Ch 5)

**Contagion** - The spread of fear or withdrawal pressure from one bank or class of banks to others. (Ch 5, Ch 6)

**Free banking** - A system with limited or no central-bank authority where banks compete and issue liabilities with relatively little centralized control. (Ch 2)

**Information asymmetry** - The condition in which depositors, creditors, or outsiders cannot cheaply judge which banks are sound and which are risky. (Ch 5)

**Lender of last resort** - The institution willing to lend in crisis to preserve liquidity and prevent wider systemic collapse. (Ch 3, Ch 7)

**Moral hazard** - The tendency for institutions to take more risk when they expect rescue or support. (Ch 3, Ch 7)

**Payments function** - The role banks play in moving funds and settling transactions across the economy. (Ch 7)

**Portfolio management function** - The role banks play in transforming and managing assets and liabilities for savers and borrowers. (Ch 7)

**Prudential supervision** - Oversight of banking conduct, balance-sheet soundness, and entry or operating conditions. (Ch 5, Ch 7)

**Twin functions of banks** - Goodhart's framing of banks as providers of both payments services and portfolio-management services. (Ch 7)
