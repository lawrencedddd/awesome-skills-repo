# Chapter 4: Bank Expansion and Fluctuation

## Core Idea
Goodhart examines how banks expand credit and how that expansion contributes to cyclical instability. The chapter strengthens his case that private incentives alone do not reliably restrain collective excess.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Collective expansion problem**:
  - When to use: when individually rational bank behavior aggregates into instability.
  - How: look at the system-wide effect of credit growth rather than any one bank's logic.
- **Fluctuation as endogenous banking outcome**:
  - When to use: when instability is blamed only on external shocks.
  - How: examine how bank competition and balance-sheet behavior amplify cycles.

## Key Concepts
- Credit expansion
- Cyclical fluctuation
- Restraint limits

## Mental Models
- A banking system can overshoot without any one bank acting irrationally in isolation.
- The question is who counteracts collective procyclicality.

## Anti-patterns
- Assuming market discipline automatically caps expansion.
- Looking for one villain when the mechanism is systemic.

## Worked Example
The chapter's point is that even if each bank pursues its interests conventionally, the aggregate result can still be excessive expansion followed by stress, creating demand for stabilizing authority.

## Key Takeaways
1. Banking cycles can be endogenous to the system.
2. Competition does not always equal restraint.
3. Systemic expansion strengthens the case for central coordination.
