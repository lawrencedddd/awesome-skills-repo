# Chapter 8: Summary and Conclusions

## Core Idea
Goodhart recapitulates the book's argument: information costs, contagion, settlement needs, and support demands naturally pull banking systems toward central-bank functions.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Functional convergence**:
  - When to use: when different historical paths seem to lead to similar institutions.
  - How: compare which functions had to be performed, regardless of formal constitutional design.
- **Central banking as cumulative response**:
  - When to use: when assessing whether the institution is overbuilt or emergent.
  - How: stack the pressures from previous chapters and see why they reinforce one another.

## Key Concepts
- Institutional convergence
- Banking-system necessity
- Coordinated support

## Mental Models
- Multiple imperfections point in the same direction.
- The emergence of central banks is a layered result, not a single-step design choice.

## Anti-patterns
- Reducing the argument to one function only.
- Treating central banking as optional after acknowledging contagion and support needs.

## Worked Example
The concluding synthesis shows how the historical and theoretical strands fit together: clearing, information, insurance limits, and support all create a cumulative case stronger than any single chapter alone.

## Key Takeaways
1. Central banking is best understood as institutional accumulation.
2. Banking stability requires more than competition.
3. The system repeatedly recreates central-bank functions when they are absent.
